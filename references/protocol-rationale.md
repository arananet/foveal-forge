# Protocol Rationale — foveal-forge

This file explains why each drill exists and what psychophysical mechanism it targets.
Every entry must cite at least one paper from `evidence.md`.

---

## D1. Fixation stability

Preparatory phase. No scored metric. Stabilises attentional state before the adaptive drills
to reduce trial-to-trial noise in subsequent threshold estimates.

## D2. Gabor contrast sensitivity

The contrast sensitivity function (CSF) peaks at mid spatial frequencies and declines
with age, particularly at high spatial frequencies relevant to near-vision tasks.
Polat et al. (2012) demonstrated that lateral-masking Gabor training improves CSF
in presbyopic adults, with training effects transferring to reading performance.
The 3-down-1-up staircase converges at the 79.4% correct threshold, the standard
psychophysical operating point for threshold estimation.

## D3. Saccade + crowding

Crowding — the impaired ability to recognise flanked targets — is a primary bottleneck
for reading in central and peripheral vision. Levi (2008) reviews the evidence that
crowding is cortical, not optical, making it trainable. The adaptive crowding-spacing
procedure reduces the critical spacing over sessions, reflecting cortical plasticity
in the ventral stream.

## D4. Reading fluency / RSVP

RSVP paradigms eliminate the need for eye movements, isolating word-level processing speed.
Calabrèse et al. (2014) validated RSVP-based reading speed assessment in low-vision
populations. The adaptive WPM procedure targets the sustained reading rate at 80%
comprehension, which is the ecologically valid operating point for most reading tasks.
